Capybara bonanza!

Here are some fun facts!!

They are the worlds largest rodent!


These adorable animals are the largest rodent in the world. They are nearly 60cm tall and weigh up to 68kg.


Their teeth don't stop growing


They have to grind their teeth down by grinding and chewing on food or bark.

They have unique vocalisations


These sounds include teeth-chattering, squealing, whining, whistling, crying, barking and clicking!

They eat plants


Capybara's are herbivores that feed primarily on aquatic plants, grasses, fruits and barks.

Oodles of Axolotls

4 fun facts about these cute little criters!

Axolotl always look like babies!


They are "neotenic" creatures, this means that even as adults they don't lose their larval features. Because of this, they never develop teeth.


They are native to only one place


There is only one place you can find Axolotls in the wild: Lake Xochimilco in southern Mexico City.

They are carnivorous


They eat many things including fish, worms, insects and crustaceans. They will eat meat that is dead or alive.

They Can Regenerate Body Parts


They can regenerate jaws, spinal cords, skin, ovary and lung tissue. They can even regenerate portions of their hearts and brains.

Superb Foxes

Fabulous facts about foxes.

Foxes can make over 40 different sounds


Foxes can produce screams, barks, and howls that can range from high pitched to lower pitched tones to communicate different things.


Foxes make use of the earth’s magnetic field to hunt


They use the Earth's magnetic field to judge the distance and direction of their prey.

People used to fear foxes but they are actually considered friendly animals


Most fox species have been seen being friendly, curious and playful whilst amongst other foxes and animals.

Foxes have impeccable hearing


They are able to hear rodents digging underground!

Coconuts Are More Dangerous Than Sharks


Coconuts falling from trees kill 30 times the amount of people that sharks do.


Shark Skin Is More Like Teeth Than Fish Scales


Their skin is made up of tiny v-shaped scales, called dermal denticles, which are made of an inner pulp cavity and an outside layer of dentine, an enamel-like material (just like our teeth).

Sharks Have A Sixth Sense


They have a unique organ called the ampullae of Lorenzini, which allows them to sense electrical stimuli and is especially helpful in hunting for hidden prey

Sharks Don’t Have Vocal Chords


Which makes them unable to make noise like other marine animals such as dolphins, whales, or seals.
